# How Much to Set Aside for Taxes as a Freelancer

**The short answer:** most freelancers should set aside **25–35% of every dollar of profit** for taxes. If you want one number to start with, **30%** is a safe default for a typical full-time freelancer — then adjust up or down once you run your real figures. Two things make up that bill: self-employment tax (a flat {(SE_TAX.totalRate * 100).toFixed(1)}% in {TAX_YEAR}) and federal income tax (which depends on your bracket), plus state income tax if your state has one.

## Why there's no single percentage

Your set-aside isn't one fixed rate because it stacks two separate taxes that behave differently:

- **Self-employment (SE) tax** is predictable. In {TAX_YEAR} it's {(SE_TAX.totalRate * 100).toFixed(1)}% — {(SE_TAX.socialSecurityRate * 100).toFixed(1)}% for Social Security (up to a wage base of ${SE_TAX.socialSecurityWageBase.toLocaleString()}) and {(SE_TAX.medicareRate * 100).toFixed(1)}% for Medicare (no cap). It applies to {(SE_TAX.netEarningsMultiplier * 100).toFixed(2)}% of your net profit.
- **Federal income tax** is progressive. A freelancer netting $30,000 sits in a low bracket; one netting $120,000 owes a much larger share. This is the part that pushes your total set-aside from the low 20s toward the mid 30s.
- **State income tax** adds anywhere from 0% (e.g. no-income-tax states) to ~13% depending on where you live.

So the honest answer is a *range*, and the only way to get your number is to run your actual income — which is what the calculator does.

## A worked example

Say you net **$60,000** in profit after expenses in {TAX_YEAR}.

- SE tax on that profit is about **${Math.round(calcSelfEmploymentTax(60000)).toLocaleString()}** (computed on {(SE_TAX.netEarningsMultiplier * 100).toFixed(2)}% of profit at {(SE_TAX.totalRate * 100).toFixed(1)}%).
- You also owe federal income tax on your profit *after* subtracting half your SE tax and your standard deduction — typically another 10–12% of profit at this income level.
- Add state tax if applicable.

For a single filer at $60k with no other income, that lands the total set-aside around **28–32% of profit** — which is why "set aside 30%" is a reasonable starting rule before you refine it.

## How to actually set the money aside

Knowing the number is useless if the cash isn't there in April. The system that works:

1. **Open a separate savings account** just for taxes. Don't commingle it with operating cash.
2. **Move your percentage off the top of every payment** — the moment a client pays you, transfer your set-aside %. Treat it as money that was never yours.
3. **Pay quarterly, not annually.** The IRS expects estimated payments through the year; saving it up but skipping the quarterly deadlines can still trigger a penalty. (See the [quarterly estimated taxes guide](/quarterly-estimated-taxes-guide).)
4. **Recalculate after a big month** so a strong quarter doesn't leave you under-saved.

## Common mistakes

- **Setting aside on revenue instead of profit** — you save on what's left *after* deductible expenses, so track expenses to avoid over-saving.
- **Forgetting state tax** — easy to budget only for federal and come up short.
- **Under-saving in year one** — new freelancers often forget SE tax entirely and get a nasty first bill.
