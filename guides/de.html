Freelancer Taxes in Germany

Last reviewed: May 2026 · By the Freelancer Tax Estimator editorial team

Germany draws a sharp legal line between two kinds of self-employment, and which side you fall on changes your registration, your taxes, and your paperwork. On top of income tax, you'll need to navigate VAT rules, quarterly advance payments, and arranging your own health insurance. This guide explains the essentials for working as a freelancer (Selbstständiger) in Germany.

Freiberufler vs. Gewerbetreibender

Germany distinguishes between Freiberufler (liberal professionals — designers, writers, engineers, doctors, lawyers, and similar) and Gewerbetreibende (commercial traders). The distinction matters: Freiberufler have simpler registration directly with the Finanzamt, are exempt from Gewerbesteuer (trade tax), and often face lighter bookkeeping. Gewerbetreibende must register a trade (Gewerbeanmeldung) and pay trade tax above an allowance. If your category is ambiguous, the Finanzamt makes the final call.

Umsatzsteuer (VAT)

VAT in Germany is generally 19% (with a reduced 7% rate for certain goods and services). You must charge and remit it unless you qualify for the Kleinunternehmerregelung (small business rule), which exempts you if your revenue is below the current threshold (historically around €22,000 in the prior year). The trade-off: as a Kleinunternehmer you don't charge VAT, but you also can't reclaim the input VAT on your own business purchases.

Filing & advance payments

You file an annual income tax return (Einkommensteuererklärung), typically due 31 July of the following year — or later (often 31 October or beyond) if a Steuerberater (tax adviser) prepares it. Once you have a tax history, the Finanzamt sets quarterly Vorauszahlungen (advance payments), usually due in March, June, September, and December, which are credited against your final assessment.

Health insurance

This is a major cost the self-employed must arrange themselves. You choose between public (gesetzliche Krankenversicherung) and private (private Krankenversicherung) cover. Premiums are partly deductible as Sonderausgaben (special expenses), but for many freelancers health and care insurance is one of the largest monthly outgoings — factor it into your rates from day one.

Key dates & thresholds at a glance

  • VAT: 19% standard / 7% reduced; Kleinunternehmer exemption below the threshold
  • Trade tax: applies to Gewerbetreibende, not Freiberufler
  • Annual return: due 31 July (later with a Steuerberater)
  • Advance payments: quarterly (March, June, September, December)

Common mistakes freelancers make

  • Assuming you're a Freiberufler when the Finanzamt classifies you as a trader.
  • Choosing the Kleinunternehmerregelung without realising you lose input VAT recovery.
  • Underpricing because health insurance wasn't built into rates.
  • Being surprised by the first round of quarterly advance payments.
