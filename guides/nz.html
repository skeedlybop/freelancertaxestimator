Freelancer Taxes in New Zealand

Last reviewed: May 2026 · By the Freelancer Tax Estimator editorial team

New Zealand keeps self-employment refreshingly simple compared with many countries — registering as a sole trader is free and fast — but there are still three things every freelancer needs to understand: provisional tax (paying in advance), the GST threshold, and ACC levies. Get these on your radar early and your first couple of tax years will be far less stressful.

Registering as a sole trader

Becoming self-employed in New Zealand is straightforward: you use your existing IRD number and manage everything through myIR online. There's no fee and no separate company to set up. Most freelancers operate as sole traders and only consider forming a company later, when turnover or liability concerns make it worthwhile.

Provisional tax

In your first year you pay your tax in a lump sum after filing. But once your residual income tax exceeds NZD $5,000, Inland Revenue moves you onto provisional tax — paying towards the current year in instalments. Under the standard option there are three instalments (typically August, January, and May), calculated at 105% of the prior year's tax (110% for larger taxpayers). Like elsewhere, the second year can pinch because you may pay last year's balance and this year's provisional tax close together.

GST registration

You must register for GST once your turnover exceeds NZD $60,000 in a 12-month period (you can register voluntarily below that). GST is charged at 15% on your services, and you file GST returns — commonly every two months or every six months. You can also claim back the GST on your business expenses, so registration isn't purely a cost.

ACC levies

This is the one newcomers often forget. Every self-employed New Zealander pays ACC (Accident Compensation Corporation) levies — roughly $1.60 per $100 of liable earnings, up to an annual cap. ACC invoices these separately from your income tax, so a bill can arrive when you're not expecting it. In return you're covered for income replacement if injury stops you working.

Key dates & thresholds at a glance

  • Tax year: 1 April – 31 March
  • Provisional tax: triggered above NZD $5,000 residual income tax; 3 instalments
  • GST: register at NZD $60,000 turnover; 15% rate
  • ACC levies: ~$1.60 per $100 of liable earnings, invoiced separately
  • IR3 return: due 7 July (later via a tax agent)

Common mistakes freelancers make

  • Forgetting ACC levies arrive as a separate bill.
  • Being caught out by the second-year provisional + residual tax overlap.
  • Not registering for GST after crossing $60,000.
  • Spending the full invoice amount without setting tax aside.
